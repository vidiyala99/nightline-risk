# Nightlife & Live-Entertainment General Liability Master Policy
**Carrier:** Admitted Market A · **Policy Year:** 2026 · **Effective:** 2026-01-01

This master policy governs general liability coverage for licensed nightlife
and live-entertainment venues operating under the Carrier's nightlife program.

## 1. Coverage — Premises Liability

### 1.1 Bodily Injury On-Premises
The Carrier shall indemnify the Insured against claims for bodily injury
arising from a covered occurrence on the insured premises, subject to the
limits stated in the Declarations and to the conditions set out in this Policy.

### 1.2 Slips, Trips, and Falls
Coverage extends to claims arising from slips, trips, and falls on insured
premises provided the Insured maintains wet-floor signage during cleaning,
documented stair lighting inspections at least monthly, and incident reports
for any prior fall in the affected area within the preceding 12 months.

### 1.3 Dance-Floor and Crowd-Surge Claims
Claims arising from injuries on a designated dance floor or from crowd surge
during a sold-out event are covered when the Insured has, at a minimum,
maintained the security-staffing ratios set forth in §4.1 and operated a
functioning capacity counter at every controlled entrance.

## 2. Coverage — Liquor Liability

### 2.1 Dram-Shop Claims
The Carrier covers dram-shop claims when the Insured can demonstrate that all
serving staff completed responsible-service training within the calendar year
and that point-of-sale records corroborate the licensee's cutoff procedures.

### 2.2 Last-Call and Cutoff Procedures
A documented last-call announcement at least 30 minutes prior to close and a
written cutoff protocol acknowledged by every bartender on shift are required
for coverage under this section.

## 3. Coverage — Assault, Battery, and Security Response

### 3.1 Third-Party Assault
Coverage applies to claims arising from third-party assault on insured premises
when the Insured can produce continuous camera footage of the affected zone
and a contemporaneous security-incident log for the date of the occurrence.

### 3.2 Use-of-Force by Security Personnel
Claims arising from use-of-force by contracted or in-house security personnel
are covered only when the Insured retains current use-of-force training
certifications for each named individual and an incident report finalized
within the timeframe in §8.1.

## 4. Conditions Precedent — Operations

### 4.1 Minimum Security Staffing
The Insured shall maintain a minimum security-staff-to-patron ratio of 1:75
during ticketed events and 1:100 during regular operations, scaled to the
licensed capacity of the premises.

### 4.2 Capacity Management
The Insured shall not exceed the licensed capacity stated in the Declarations
at any time. A real-time entrance counter and exit counter shall be operated
during all hours the premises are open to the public.

### 4.3 Door Identification Scanning
Every patron shall be ID-scanned at a controlled entrance during ticketed
events. Scan logs shall be retained for not less than 90 days and produced on
the Carrier's request in connection with any open claim.

## 5. Conditions Precedent — Evidence Preservation

### 5.1 Camera Coverage of Controlled Zones
The Insured shall operate camera coverage over every controlled entrance,
exit, bar service area, and dance floor. Coverage gaps in any controlled zone
constitute a condition violation and may suspend coverage for occurrences in
the affected zone until the gap is remediated.

### 5.2 POS and Door-Scan Record Retention
Point-of-sale transaction logs and door-scan logs shall be retained for not
less than 90 days and shall be provided to the Carrier within 10 business days
of a written request in connection with an open or pending claim.

## 7. Camera Footage Preservation

### 7.1 Continuous Recording Requirement
Camera systems covering controlled zones (entrance, exit, bar, dance floor,
service corridors) shall record continuously during operating hours and for
not less than two hours before opening and after close.

### 7.2 Retention Window
Footage shall be preserved for a minimum of ninety (90) days from the date of
capture. Footage relevant to a reported incident shall be preserved until the
underlying claim is closed or three (3) years from the date of occurrence,
whichever is later.

### 7.3 Camera Footage Continuity During Outage
When a primary camera covering a controlled zone is degraded, offline, or
otherwise impaired, the Insured shall preserve alternative footage from an
adjacent fixed-position camera, body-worn camera, or a documented outage
record describing the cause, duration, and remediation of the impairment.
Failure to preserve alternative footage or to record the outage shall be
treated as a coverage gap under §5.1 for the affected zone and time window,
and may impair claims defensibility for any incident reported in that window.

### 7.4 Tampering and Deletion
Deletion, overwriting, or alteration of footage within the retention window
in §7.2 constitutes a material breach of this Policy. The Carrier reserves
all rights, including rescission, with respect to any claim affected by such
tampering or deletion.

## 8. Incident Reporting

### 8.1 Manager Countersignature Requirement
Every incident report involving bodily injury, assault, battery, ejection,
medical response, or property damage shall be finalized and countersigned by
the duty manager on shift within seven (7) calendar days of occurrence. Final
reports shall be retained for not less than three (3) years from the date of
occurrence and shall be produced to the Carrier on written request.

### 8.2 Carrier Notification
The Insured shall notify the Carrier of any incident reasonably expected to
give rise to a claim within fourteen (14) calendar days of occurrence. Late
notice may, at the Carrier's election, reduce or void coverage for the
affected occurrence.

### 8.3 Independent Witness Statements
Where reasonably available, the Insured shall obtain a written witness
statement from at least one independent witness for any incident reported
under §8.1. Witness statements shall be attached to the final incident report.

## 9. Exclusions

### 9.1 Excluded Activities — Pyrotechnics and Open Flame
Bodily injury or property damage arising from indoor pyrotechnic displays,
open-flame stage effects, or unsupervised use of haze or fog machines outside
manufacturer specifications is excluded from coverage under this Policy.

### 9.2 Excluded Activities — Unpermitted Capacity Events
Occurrences arising from events at which the licensed capacity of the
premises was exceeded by more than 10% at any time are excluded from coverage,
without regard to the cause of the occurrence.

### 9.3 Excluded Conduct — Known Intoxication
Claims arising from service to a patron the Insured's staff knew or should
reasonably have known was visibly intoxicated at the time of service are
excluded under the liquor liability section.

### 9.4 Excluded Losses — Force Majeure
Property damage arising from acts of God, including but not limited to
earthquake, flood, named windstorm, and civil unrest, is excluded.

## 10. Definitions

### 10.1 Controlled Zone
Any area of the insured premises in which the Insured operates capacity
management, ID scanning, security staffing, or beverage service. Includes
entrances, exits, dance floors, bar service areas, and back-of-house corridors
used by patrons.

### 10.2 Independent Witness
An individual present at the time of an occurrence who is not employed by,
contracted to, or a relative of any employee of the Insured.
